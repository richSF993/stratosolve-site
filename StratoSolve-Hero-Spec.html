Section 4 — Layout Hierarchy & Developer Notes
Visual hierarchy — critical
The headline must be the largest, heaviest element on the page. The company name appears ONLY as a logo in the nav — never as text in the hero. An executive landing on this page should read the message before they read the brand name.
Alignment
All hero content is left-aligned, not centered. Left alignment reads as authoritative and editorial. Centered hero text reads as marketing. This audience is C-suite — left-align everything.
Headline line breaks
The headline breaks across 5 lines on desktop as shown in the mockup. Allow it to reflow naturally on mobile — do not force line breaks on small screens. The italic gold emphasis on "the other side of it." must be preserved.
Stats disclaimer
The four credential stats (12+, 0%, 125%, 4) reflect Richard's prior career at 8x8, Inc. — not StratoSolve client results. A small disclaimer must appear either below the stats or in the Founder section. See Word document for exact disclaimer copy.
No stock photography
The background must be the solid navy color #0f1f3d — not a stock image. The transmission tower / sunset image currently on the live site must be removed. A subtle grid pattern overlay is optional.
Mobile behavior
On mobile: nav collapses to hamburger menu, headline scales down to 36–44px, stats stack 2x2, CTA buttons stack vertically. Headshot image (if added) hides on mobile — copy only on small screens.
